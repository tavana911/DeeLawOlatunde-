Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to the questions we hear most from new clients. For specific legal advice, schedule a consultation.

Ask Us Directly

How do I know if I need a lawyer?

+
If you are involved in a legal dispute, signing a significant contract, navigating a property transaction, going through a divorce, or facing any situation where your rights, assets, or family may be affected — consulting a lawyer is strongly advisable. Early legal advice can prevent small issues from becoming costly problems.

How much do your services cost?

+
Fees vary depending on the nature and complexity of your matter. We offer an initial consultation to assess your situation and provide a clear fee proposal before any work commences. We believe in transparency — you will always know the cost before we proceed.

What happens during the initial consultation?

+
The initial consultation is a confidential meeting where you describe your legal issue in detail. We will assess the merits of your case, explain your legal options, outline likely timelines and costs, and recommend a course of action. This helps us both determine whether we are the right fit for your matter.

How long does a typical case take?

+
Timelines depend greatly on the type and complexity of the case. Mediated disputes may resolve in weeks, while High Court litigation can take one to three years or more. We always pursue the most efficient route to a favourable outcome and will provide a realistic timeline assessment at the outset.

Is my information kept confidential?

+
Absolutely. All client communications and case information are protected by strict attorney-client privilege and our professional ethical obligations under the Rules of Professional Conduct for Legal Practitioners. Your information is never disclosed without your explicit consent.

Can you handle matters outside Lagos?

+
Yes. We regularly handle matters across multiple Nigerian states and can appear before state High Courts, the Federal High Court, and the Court of Appeal in relevant jurisdictions. We also collaborate with experienced correspondents in jurisdictions where required.

What is ADR and when is it appropriate?

+
ADR encompasses mediation, arbitration, and negotiation — methods of resolving disputes outside the traditional court system. It is often faster, less expensive, more private, and preserves relationships better than litigation. It is particularly appropriate for commercial disputes, contract matters, and family law issues where parties prefer a collaborative rather than adversarial process.

How do I get started?

+
Simply complete the consultation request form on this page, call us directly, or send an email. We typically respond within one business day to schedule your confidential initial consultation. The sooner you reach out, the sooner we can begin protecting your interests.
